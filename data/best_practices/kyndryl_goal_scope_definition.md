Goal Definition
The overall goal of this study is to assess the environmental impacts of IT hardware in Kyndryl’s owned data centers, from cradle-to-grave, to identify areas for circularity improvements. Specific goals include:
Develop a baseline for LCA methodology for IT data center hardware
Inform Kyndryl’s product sourcing decisions
This report is designed for Kyndryl Holdings, Inc. The study was initiated by Northwestern University. The study leverages existing Product Carbon Footprints for climate change impact calculations.
Scope Definition
Product System: IT hardware in Kyndryl’s owned datacenters. In scope hardware includes GPUs, rackservers, Hard disk drives (HDD) and Solid State Drives (SSD).
Functional Unit: One hardware component operating for five years
System Boundary:
Functional boundary: Raw materials extraction, manufacturing, operating use, end of life – cradle to grave 
*Transportation is excluded from functional boundary due to the complexities in data collection
Geographical boundary: Calculating impact assuming a Kyndryl- owned datacenter in Canada. Leverages site specific data from Project North Star provided by Kyndryl
Allocation Procedures: Economic allocation, the assessment will allocate environmental impacts based on the economic value or market price of the co-products or outputs.
Impact Categories: The impact categories that will be assessed include water use (m3), resource or energy use (MJ ), and climate change (kg CO2e)
