## Group A — Explicit in Kyndryl draft

### A1. Goal of the study
**OEM content:** To analyze and evaluate the environmental impact of its products based on international standards (ISO 14040 and ISO 14044); the SDP* evaluates the entire product lifecycle, including manufacturing, distribution, usage, and disposal, to precisely measure environmental impacts; results will be utilized to improve product environmental specifications and inform product development (p. 1).
**Kyndryl draft:** Assess environmental impacts of IT hardware in Kyndryl's owned data centers, cradle-to-grave, to identify circularity improvement areas. Sub-goals: develop a baseline LCA methodology for IT data center hardware; inform Kyndryl's product sourcing decisions.
**Comparison:** Aligned on goal structure (lifecycle-wide impact assessment to inform sourcing/development), but Samsung's scope is product design optimization; Kyndryl's is circularity and data center baseline.

### A2. Intended audience & commissioner
**OEM content:** Not explicitly stated. Document does not identify commissioner or intended primary audience. Appears to be internal or regulatory documentation based on methodological rigor and disclosure level (p. 1).
**Kyndryl draft:** Designed for Kyndryl Holdings, Inc. Initiated by Northwestern University.
**Comparison:** OEM silent on audience and commissioner.

### A3. Product system / scope of hardware
**OEM content:** Galaxy Chromebook 3 360 (model XQ520QHA), complete with product and accessories; includes inputs/outputs in manufacturing processes, distribution routes, and entire product lifecycle (p. 1); system boundary includes manufacturing (raw materials and product assembly), distribution (Vietnam to KR), use (4 years), and disposal (waste treatment of parts and materials) (p. 1).
**Kyndryl draft:** IT hardware in Kyndryl's owned data centers. In scope: GPUs, rack servers, HDDs, SSDs.
**Comparison:** Different product classes (Samsung reports consumer laptop; Kyndryl focuses on data center components). Samsung treats entire assembled product as one system; clear.

### A4. Functional unit
**OEM content:** Not explicitly stated. Implied from "Life Cycle Assessment for Galaxy Chromebook 3 360(KR)" and use-phase assumption: 4 years use (p. 1). No quantified service unit (e.g., "1 device-year" or "computational throughput").
**Kyndryl draft:** "One hardware component operating for five years."
**Comparison:** Partially aligned on time period (Samsung: 4 years; Kyndryl: 5 years). Neither explicitly quantifies the functional unit as delivered performance (service).

### A5. System boundary — lifecycle stages
**OEM content:** Includes manufacturing (raw materials and product assembly by Samsung Electronics), distribution (Vietnam to KR), use (4 years), and disposal (waste treatment of parts and materials). Stages explicitly listed in "System boundary of LCA" table (p. 1).
**Kyndryl draft:** Cradle-to-grave: raw materials extraction, manufacturing, operating use, end-of-life. **Transportation explicitly excluded** due to data collection complexity.
**Comparison:** Different on transportation: Samsung includes distribution transport (Vietnam to KR) (p. 1); Kyndryl explicitly excludes transportation. Both include manufacturing, use, and EoL.

### A6. Geographic boundary
**OEM content:** Distribution sourced from Vietnam to KR (Korea implied by "KR" notation) (p. 1). Use-phase geography: South Korea (KR). No explicit statement of grid mix or supply chain manufacturing region.
**Kyndryl draft:** Kyndryl-owned data center in Canada. Site-specific data from Project North Star.
**Comparison:** Different geographies (Samsung: Korea; Kyndryl: Canada). Samsung does not state whether it uses region-specific grid mix or global averages for manufacturing.

### A7. Allocation procedure
**OEM content:** Not stated in document (p. 1–2).
**Kyndryl draft:** Economic allocation, based on market price of co-products.
**Comparison:** OEM silent on allocation method.

### A8. Impact categories
**OEM content:** 11 environmental impact categories: climate change, acidification, ecotoxicity (freshwater), ecotoxicity (marine), ecotoxicity (terrestrial), energy resource depletion, eutrophication, human toxicity, material resource depletion (metals/minerals), ozone depletion, photochemical oxidant formation (p. 1). Characterized environment impact shown for all categories; global warming impact profile focuses on climate change (p. 2).
**Kyndryl draft:** Water use (m³), resource/energy use (MJ), climate change (kg CO₂e).
**Comparison:** OEM goes far beyond Kyndryl's draft: 11 categories vs 3. OEM includes acidification, ecotoxicity (3 types), eutrophication, human toxicity, ozone depletion, photochemical oxidant formation. Kyndryl covers only climate change, water, and energy.

## Group B — Implied or partial in Kyndryl draft

### B1. Data sources — primary vs secondary
**OEM content:** Database: Ecoinvent 3.10 (p. 1). No statement of primary data collection ratio, supplier-specific data, or cutoff year. LCA software: SDP (Sustainability Data Platform) (p. 1).
**Kyndryl draft:** Implied secondary. Leverages existing Product Carbon Footprints for climate change impact calculations.
**Comparison:** Both rely on secondary databases. Samsung names Ecoinvent 3.10; Kyndryl does not specify database. No statement from either on primary/secondary ratio or cutoff year.

### B2. Use-phase assumptions
**OEM content:** Use phase: 4 years (p. 1). No explicit statement of power draw (TDP), utilization rate, duty cycle, or grid carbon intensity (kg CO₂e/kWh). Use-phase impact shown as percentage in Global Warming Impact Profile but not itemized with parameters.
**Kyndryl draft:** Partial. 5-year lifetime stated (via FU). Use-phase grid mix implied via Canada/Project North Star site data, but no explicit utilization rate, TDP, or workload assumption.
**Comparison:** Both state lifetime but not power/utilization. Samsung: 4 years; Kyndryl: 5 years. Neither discloses grid mix, TDP, or duty cycle assumptions.

### B3. End-of-life modeling approach
**OEM content:** Disposal stage included: "Waste treatment of parts and material" (p. 1). No explicit statement of recycling rate, avoided-burden methodology, recycled-content credit, or EoL allocation method.
**Kyndryl draft:** EoL is in scope, but the modeling approach (cut-off vs recycled content vs avoided burden) is not specified.
**Comparison:** Both include EoL in scope but do not specify the modeling approach (cut-off, avoided burden, or PEF Circular Footprint Formula).

## Gaps vs Kyndryl draft
- **B1 (database source):** Kyndryl draft is silent on which secondary database to use; Samsung names Ecoinvent 3.10. Actionable gap for Kyndryl.
- **C1 (standards conformance):** Kyndryl draft does not state conformance to ISO 14040/44; Samsung explicitly claims both.
- **C2 (LCIA characterization model):** Kyndryl draft lists impact categories but names no method; Samsung specifies CML v4.8 + IPCC.
- **A8 (impact categories):** Kyndryl draft covers only 3 categories (water, energy, climate); Samsung reports 11. Significant gap for Kyndryl's scope.

---

## Goes beyond Kyndryl draft
- **A8 (expanded impact categories):** Samsung's 11-category approach (acidification, ecotoxicity variants, eutrophication, human toxicity, ozone depletion, photochemical oxidants) exceeds Kyndryl's 3-category plan. Relevant if Kyndryl later expands beyond climate/water/energy.
- **C2 (named LCIA method):** Samsung explicitly chooses CML v4.8 with IPCC; Kyndryl draft names no method. Provides concrete precedent.
- **C1 (ISO 14040/44 conformance):** Samsung's explicit claim offers a model for Kyndryl's conformance statement.

---

## Notable absences in OEM doc
- **A7 (allocation procedure):** No statement on economic vs physical allocation for co-products.
- **B1 (primary/secondary data ratio):** No disclosure of proportion of supplier-measured vs database data; no cutoff year for Ecoinvent 3.10.
- **B2 (use-phase granularity):** No power draw (TDP), utilization rate, duty cycle, or grid intensity (kg CO₂e/kWh) given; use phase is 4 years but absent from functional-unit statement.
- **B3 (EoL modeling):** Disposal is in scope but approach (cut-off vs avoided burden vs recycled content credit) not specified.
- **C3 (cut-off thresholds):** No <1% mass or energy cut-off rule stated.
- **C4 (temporal boundary):** Reference year for foreground and background data not given.
- **C5 (data quality assessment):** No pedigree matrix or qualitative narrative on geographic/temporal representativeness or consistency.
- **C6 (uncertainty & sensitivity):** Acknowledged as a limitation ("will be continuously updated and improved") but not quantified.
- **C7 (third-party review):** No critical review or verification name given.
- **C9 (circularity metrics):** No recycled content %, recyclability %, reuse rate, or take-back program coverage stated (expected absence per scope).
